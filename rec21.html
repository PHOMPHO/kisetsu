Kisetsv (/hisetsʊ/) is the spirit of the seasons, being able to control the temperature and weather depending on their mood he lives mostly hidden in the Shenkuu mountains which houses the shrines and temples dedicated to him and his siblings, the spirit of the wind and the spirit of the sea.

According to the legends, all three of them where born in the beginning of time itself, the colorful wind gained the shape of a dragon bound by the air, the sparkling waters gained the form of a dragon bound by the waters, and the flowerbeds gained the form of a dragon bound by the earth. The people believe these three deities are the guardians of each plane of existence, the divine plane where the Gods rest; the astral plane where the dead reside; and the physical plane where the humans dwell.

Kisetsv is a calm and gentle creature that keeps themselves mostly outside of the human gaze, living mostly in solitude inside the dense forests that surround Shenkuu. Protective of the nature and the other creatures in the forest he tries to maintain the balance between the seasons to help the growth of those that live in the physical plane.

However, they can be moody from time to time as well, making the weather and, consequently, the seasons around them change even if they don't necessarily mean to cause such havoc. Things seen as 'small' or 'insignificant' by humans are generally what causes such changes, like the destruction of nature or not praying respect o the deities protecting them from disasters.

To those closer to him like their siblings and the other deities in the Shenkuu pantheon, Kisetsv is seem as a bit childish and impulsive in his decisions to help or abdicate from acting on something regarding the humans. After all, between the other deities they are the youngest among them all.

When happy, he tends to skip around the forest and bloom flowers that may not even be in season, to the human world this is seen as a gift from the deities, a signal that they are happy with their offers. On the other hand, when stressed or sad he tends to sulk inside his temple and consequently the temperatures dawns and light snow may cover the temple itself, acting almost like a protective barrier to keep others outside.
